Top 10 Books on Physics

1.The Feynman Lectures on Physics" by Richard P. Feynman- This classic set of lectures provides a comprehensive introduction to physics.

2. "Principles of Physics" by Resnick, Halliday, and Walker - A widely used and respected textbook that covers classical physics.

3. "Introduction to the Theory of Relativity" by Peter G. Bergmann - A great introduction to Einstein's theory of relativity.

4. "Six Easy Pieces: Essentials of Physics By Its Most Brilliant Teacher" by Richard P. Feynman - A more accessible version of Feynman's lectures.

5. "Quantum Mechanics and Path Integrals" by Richard P. Feynman - A deep dive into the world of quantum mechanics.

6. "Cosmos" by Carl Sagan - While not a physics textbook, this book explores the wonders of the universe and the science behind it.

7. "A Brief History of Time" by Stephen Hawking - A popular science book that discusses cosmology, black holes, and the nature of the universe.

8. "The Elegant Universe: Superstrings, Hidden Dimensions, and the Quest for the Ultimate Theory" by Brian Greene - A book that explores the cutting edge of theoretical physics, including string theory.

9. "Black Holes and Time Warps: Einstein's Outrageous Legacy" by Kip S. Thorne - An in-depth look at the physics of black holes and the theory of general relativity.

10. "QED: The Strange Theory of Light and Matter" by Richard P. Feynman - A concise exploration of quantum electrodynamics, one of the most accurate theories in all of science.

Top 10 Books on English

1.The Feynman Lectures on Physics" by Richard P. Feynman- This classic set of lectures provides a comprehensive introduction to physics.

2. "Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid" by Douglas R. Hofstadter - An exploration of the relationships between mathematics, art, and music, drawing from the works of Gödel, Escher, and Bach.

3. "The Princeton Companion to Mathematics" edited by Timothy Gowers - An extensive reference book providing an overview of various mathematical topics and their importance.

4. "How to Prove It: A Structured Approach" by Daniel J. Velleman - A beginner-friendly book that teaches the fundamentals of mathematical proof and logic.

5. "The Art of Mathematics" by Jerry P. King - A collection of essays that showcase the beauty, creativity, and elegance of mathematics.

6. "Introduction to the Theory of Computation" by Michael Sipser - A comprehensive introduction to theoretical computer science and the mathematical foundations of computation.

7. "Flatland: A Romance of Many Dimensions" by Edwin A. Abbott - A classic novella that introduces readers to the concept of higher dimensions in a creative and accessible way.

8. "Euler's Gem: The Polyhedron Formula and the Birth of Topology" by David S. Richeson - Explores the history of topology and its connections to geometry through the story of Euler's polyhedron formula.

9. "A Course of Pure Mathematics" by G.H. Hardy - A classic text on pure mathematics, covering topics such as calculus, number theory, and mathematical analysis.

10. "Prime Obsession: Bernhard Riemann and the Greatest Unsolved Problem in Mathematics" by John Derbyshire - Explores the intriguing story of the Riemann Hypothesis, one of the most famous unsolved problems in mathematics..
